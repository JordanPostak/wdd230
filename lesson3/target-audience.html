Jordan Postak

Overview

Audience

The Sandpoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website is designed to appeal to a specific target audience - local businesses and community members who are interested in promoting and supporting the economic growth and development of Sandpoint. The website is a valuable resource for local business owners who are seeking information about available resources and services to help them grow their business, as well as for community members who want to learn about upcoming events and opportunities to get involved in the local business community. Additionally, the website serves as a platform for promoting tourism and attracting new businesses and residents to the area. As such, the target audience of the Sandpoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website includes local business owners, entrepreneurs, community leaders, and residents who are interested in supporting the economic vitality and overall success of the Sandpoint area.

Personas

Outdoor enthusiast:

This persona is an avid outdoors person who enjoys hiking, biking, and skiing. They are interested in exploring new places and trying new activities.

Business owner:

This persona owns a small business in SandPoint and is looking to network with other local business owners. They want to stay up to date on local events and initiatives that may impact their business.

Persona Three:

This persona has recently retired and moved to SandPoint to enjoy a slower pace of life. They are interested in community events and activities, and want to get involved with local organizations.

Scenarios

Scenario One:

The outdoor enthusiast is planning a winter skiing trip to SandPoint and visits the Chamber of Commerce website to find information about local ski resorts. They navigate to the "Winter Activities" section of the website on the Discover page, where they find information about local ski resorts, including trail maps, snow conditions, and rental equipment. They also find a list of recommended accommodations in the area, as well as information about other winter activities, such as snowmobiling and ice fishing.

Scenario Two:

The outdoor enthusiast is interested in exploring the natural beauty of SandPoint and visits the SandPoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website to learn about local parks and wildlife areas. They navigate to the "Discover" section of the website, where they find information about local parks, wildlife areas, and scenic drives. They also find a list of guided tours and outdoor activities, such as birdwatching and photography classes.

Scenario Three:

The business owner visits the SandPoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website to learn about local marketing opportunities. They navigate to the "Join" section of the website, where they find information about local advertising opportunities, such as sponsoring community events and advertising in the local newspaper. They also find a list of marketing resources, such as local marketing consultants and social media experts.

Scenario Four:

The business owner is looking to hire new employees and visits the SandPoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website to learn about local job fairs and recruiting events. They navigate to the "Join" page, where they find information about local businesses, recruiting events, and job postings. They are able to post their own job openings on the website and receive applications from qualified candidates.

Scenario Five:

The retiree is interested in exploring the arts and culture of SandPoint and visits the SandPoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website to learn about local museums and galleries. They navigate to the "Discover" section of the website, where they find information about local museums, galleries, and theaters. They also find a list of upcoming art exhibits, musical performances, and other cultural events.

Scenario Six:

The retiree is interested in learning more about the history of SandPoint and visits the SandPoint Idaho Chamber of Commerce website to learn about local historical sites and landmarks. They navigate to the "Discover" section of the website, where they find information about local historical sites, including museums, monuments, and landmarks. They also find a list of guided historical tours and educational programs, such as lectures and workshops.
